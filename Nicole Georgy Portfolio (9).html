About

Nicole Georgy is a graduate of UEA’s MA in Creative Writing. A Pushcart Prize nominee, her short fiction (published under a pen name) appears in over a dozen publications, including The New Orleans Review and New Letters. Her novel What We See in the Dark was longlisted for Mslexia’s 2024 Novel Award. She won second prize in the Lilith Fiction Contest 2024 and honourable mention in Moment Magazine’s Karma Fiction Contest 2025.

What We See in the Dark

Thriller · 1999 Crete & Present-Day England

When a tarot reader experiences terrifying visions of the mother she never knew, she and her twin travel to Crete to find her before the prophecy destroys them all. Set between 1999 Crete and present-day England, a gothic novel about sisterhood, obsession, and the dangerous consequences of trying to outrun fate.

New Novel

The Singing Rocks

Thriller · Magical Realism · Coastal Kent

When compulsive-swimmer Andie finds a drowned woman on the beach of her tiny British coastal town, the police call it an accident. But Andie, unafraid of the sea and what watches from the rocks, isn't so sure. The dead woman swam the same waters, at the same hour, and someone — or something — seems very determined that Andie stop asking questions. To find the truth, she must understand why East Mores is so dangerous for women like her, and why it has started pulling her towards the sea.
